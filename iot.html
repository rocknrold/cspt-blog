What is the Internet of Things?

The Internet of Things (IoT) is a broad set of technologies and use cases with no clear definition. One viable perspective defines IoT as the use of network-connected devices embedded in the physical environment to improve an existing process or to enable a previously unimaginable scenario.

The specific use cases and opportunities across various industries are numerous, and the world of IoT is only getting started in many ways. These scenarios reveal a set of common challenges and patterns. When compared to other cloud-centric technology applications.

What are the IoT platforms?

The Internet of Things (IoT) aims to connect devices remotely for smooth operation and ease of use. An IoT platform connects device sensors to a data network. It provides information about the data used in the backend application. An IoT platform is a collection of components that enables developers to distribute applications, collect data remotely, secure connectivity, and execute scripts.

An IoT platform manages device connectivity and enables developers to create new mobile software applications. It simplifies data collection from devices and enables business transformation. It connects various components, ensuring a continuous flow of communication between the devices.

IoT will aid in better understanding of customer needs and the development of products that meet those needs. It will give organizations more intelligence and visibility into operations, allowing for better decision-making.

Device Platforms

The specific hardware available to you for developing IoT applications is enormously diverse. This variety begins with the hardware platform options. Platforms include single-board computers like the Beaglebone and Raspberry Pi, as well as microcontroller platforms like the Arduino series, Particle boards, and the Adafruit Feather.

Protecting Data in IoT

As more employees adopt the digital nomad lifestyle, the proliferation of devices on corporate networks adds to security complexity at the application level. And all of these applications must adhere to the organization’s security policies in order to keep users, their devices, and the network safe. Security can be both an impediment to and an enabler of IoT service development and adoption.

FortiNAC

FortiNAC simplifies the deployment of IoT devices by automating most of the authentication process using a sponsor. When a new IoT device tries to connect to the network, FortiNAC automatically places the device in an isolated network, profiles the device, and sends the information and the suspected type of device to the appropriate department for review and authorization. Once the device is confirmed, FortiNAC notifies the firewall of the type of device and where to place it in the correct network segment. The solution is also easy to upgrade and scale across organizations of all sizes and industries
