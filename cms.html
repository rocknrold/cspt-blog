What is a Content Management System?

A content management system (CMS) is an application that enables various contributors to develop, update, and publish web content. In most cases, content in a CMS is stored in a database and presented in a presentation layer based on a series of models.

In an organization Content Management Systems are used in many different purposes such as publishing documents and managing organizations data the also enables them to share information mainly images, content such as blogs/articles, videos and any web content you can think of. And in an organization CMS where typically managed by the digital team.

CMS writers create your content and ensure that it appears properly on your web assets. CMS writers are also known as content creators, web producers, or web design professionals in some organization. These individuals maintain the content management system, ensure the consistency of the content after it is published, ensure that it looks as intended, and that all ties function properly.

(Leibtag, The Dream Digital Team 2013)

Misconception about CMS

Although different CMSs provide varying levels of functionality and features, a typical CMS is not:

  • An authoring tool. It is not a text, graphics, video, or audio manipulation tool.
  • A tool for automatically organizing or structuring content for you. Although some CMSs have modules or work with third-party software suppliers to provide tools to try and automatically categorize and structure content.
  • A transaction processing tool. CMSs do not process transactions in the way an e-commerce engine does.
  • An analytics platform. Although most CMSs will give you analytical reporting both on how the system is being used and how users are requesting the content it manages and serves, a CMS will typically work with other analytics tools to meet reporting and analysis requirements.
  • Only a publishing tool. Of course, a CMS is a publishing tool, but it is much more than that, which is sometimes overlooked.

(Friedlein, Content Management Systems (CMS) 2007)

Software as a Service (SaaS) Content Management System (CMS) or SaaS CMS

In a quick overview of what is a SaaS CMS, it is a software that operates exclusively in the cloud rather than on-premise. For instance, with an on-premise CMS, the solution is built on your company’s infrastructure and handled by the IT team. The solution is delivered as a service with a SaaS CMS, eliminating the need for both tech infrastructure and teams to support the infrastructure.
